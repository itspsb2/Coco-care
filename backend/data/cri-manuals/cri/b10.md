### Advisory Circular No B 10: STEM BLEEDING DISEASE AND ITS CONTROL

Stem bleeding is a common disease in every coconut growing area. It can occur due to several causes, but if it is due to the fungus *Ceratocystis paradoxa*, prompt action has to be taken to treat the palms.

#### Symptoms of the disease
- The main symptom is the oozing of a reddish-brown, rust-coloured liquid from longitudinal cracks on the bark.
- Bleeding points turn brown and finally to black.
- The tissues beneath the bleeding point turn yellow and decay to a brown fibrous mass.
- Adjacent bleeding points coalesce beneath the bark and form large decayed areas.
- With time, bleeding ceases and the liquid dries on the bark as black patches.
- Young palms with rapidly expanding trunk bases are more prone due to cracking of the bark.

#### Other causes for stem bleeding
- **Ganoderma infection:** Often shows basal stem bleeding. It is rare in Sri Lanka, and the production of bracket-shaped fruiting bodies of the fungus at the base of the palm is a characteristic sign.
- **Other causes:** Lightning damage, fire damage, use of high doses of fertilizer, flooding, frequently fluctuating water tables, and red weevil attack.
    - Fire and lightning damage can be identified by the presence of ash and a cluster of palms with broken fronds, respectively.
    - Red weevil infested palms can be identified by the presence of empty cocoons and circular holes on the trunk and leaf petioles.

#### Control measures
Early identification makes management easy.
- **Fungal Bleeding:** Affected tissues should be removed with a little healthy tissue, and the wound should be treated with Bordeaux paste, Bordeaux mixture, or 1% copper fungicide solution. After a few weeks, when there is no more bleeding, the wound can be dressed with coal tar to prevent termite damage.
- **Lightning Damage:** An oblique hole should be cut into the trunk to facilitate draining out of collected fluid. Later, these holes should be closed with a cement-sand mixture.
- **Red Weevil Infestation:** Monocrotophos insecticide should be injected into the trunk to kill the larvae feeding on soft tissues inside.

**Bordeaux Paste:** Prepared by dissolving 450 g Copper Sulphate in 2.25 l of water and mixing with a suspension of 450 g of quick lime in 2.25 l of water.

**Bordeaux Mixture:** (Use earthenware vessels)
- Copper sulphate: 200 g
- Quick lime: 200 g
- Water: 25 litres

Dissolve copper sulphate overnight in 5 litres of water (suspend in a bag to facilitate dissolving). Suspend lime separately in 20 litres of water and strain through a fine cloth. Add the copper sulphate to the lime, stirring vigorously. Use immediately after preparation.

**Copper Fungicides:** Many brands are available. Prepare a 1% solution according to the manufacturer's instructions.

**Note:** Bordeaux mixture, paste, and fungicides are toxic. Handle with care.
