### Advisory Circular No B 7: MAMMALIAN PESTS OF COCONUT

Rats, bandicoots, porcupines, and bats are the common mammals that attack coconut palms.

### A. Rats
Rats damage nuts and coconut seedlings.
- **In bearing palms:** They gnaw into nuts, feeding on the kernel and nut water. Tender nuts (3-8 months old) are the most vulnerable. Unopened inflorescences are also known to be attacked. Varieties like 'Nawasi' and King Coconut appear to have a greater attraction to rats.
- **In seedlings:** Rats gnaw at the base and feed on the soft tissues in the bud region, resulting in withering, drying up of the bud, and death. This can occur in nurseries and in transplanted seedlings.

#### Control
**1. Estate Sanitation:** Weedy lands with debris (logs, stumps, husk heaps) are ideal breeding habitats. Remove or burn such material and keep weeds under check.

**2. Traps:** Use baits like roasted bread, copra, and partly burnt coconut kernel.
- **Crown traps:** Set up in the crown of the palm, using a pulley fixed to the fronds to hoist or lower the trap.
- **Ground traps:** Set up in nurseries and transplanted fields near the openings of rat burrows. Precautions must be taken to prevent hazards to workmen and domestic animals.

**3. Barriers:**
- **Tree banding:** Fix a sheet of aluminum or galvanized iron (0.3m broad) around the trunk, about 1 metre from the ground. The wire for fixing should not be wound spirally (which would allow rats to climb). Fronds of banded palms should not touch neighbouring trees or buildings. Clear crowns of rats and nests prior to banding.
- **For young seedlings:** Cover the basal part with chicken mesh tightened at both ends. Alternatively, arrange 3-4 roof tiles ("Sinhala ulu") vertically leaning against the stem, with the top tied and the base covered with soil.

**4. Chemical Control:**
- Rodenticides are generally used in baits. Use pre-baiting (unpoisoned bait for 3-4 days) before offering poisoned bait. Wax blocks impregnated with rodenticide are a more convenient method.
- **Crown baiting:** Place plastic bags containing poisoned bait in the axil of a frond where fruit bunches are vulnerable. Make a small slit in the bag. Monthly placements may be necessary. Inspect periodically for spillage and bury any that is found.
- **Ground baiting:** Place poisoned bait in receptacles (like coconut shells) in the field. Cover with a metal sheet or wooden box allowing only rats access, protecting it from rain and domestic animals.

### B. Bandicoots
Bandicoots feed on the basal part of seedlings in nurseries and transplanted fields.
- **Symptoms:** Withering or drying up of the bud. The feeding site is on the base near ground level or under the soil. The attack is almost always fatal.

**Control:** The control measures recommended for rats (estate sanitation, ground traps, and ground baiting) are applicable.

### C. Porcupines
In young plantations near jungles, porcupines destroy seedlings and young palms by feeding on the base, causing damage similar to bandicoots. Replanting may become necessary in some areas.

**Control:** Erect fences to individual seedlings or protect fields with a fence along the forest border. Cover the base of seedlings with chicken mesh.

### D. Bats
Bats are a seasonal pest that feed on tender coconuts when soft fruits are not available. They are a localized problem near their roosting places.
- **Symptoms:** Tender nuts fall prematurely. Damaged leaflets (mostly in the upper whorl) appear tattered and bleached.

**Control:** The only practical remedy is shooting them whilst roosting during the day. Lighting crackers may scare them and prevent revisits for short periods.
