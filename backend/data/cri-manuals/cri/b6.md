### Advisory Circular No B 6: TERMITES AND THEIR CONTROL

Termites are found in almost all coconut growing areas. Some live in soil around roots, and some live in mounds. They are social insects living in colonies and are usually abundant in clay soils.

#### Nature of damage and identification
Two species, *Odontotermes obesus* and *Odontotermes redemanni*, damage coconut seedlings in nurseries and soon after transplanting.
- **Signs:** The first visible sign is wilting of the central shoot (bud leaf), which easily comes out when pulled.
- **Damage:** Termites attack the husk around the seed nut and enter the nut at the base of the collar. Damaged husk is visible if the seedling is removed. If the nut and stem are split open, sand incrustations are a distinguishable feature. On clay soil, losses can be up to 20% of seedlings.
- **Other Species:**
    - *Nasutitermes ceylonicus*: Nests in roots, constructs runways on the trunk, and feeds on bark tissues, weakening the palm.
    - *Odontotermes horni*: Lives in close contact with roots, feeds on mulching materials, and occasionally attacks the root system.

#### Control
A high standard of estate sanitation is the basis of prevention.
- Collect and burn fallen leaves, useless nuts, and dispose of dead trees properly.
- Destroy termite mounds and kill the queen termite (which has a large, swollen, creamy-white abdomen and grub-like appearance).
- Remove termite-damaged mulch immediately. Avoid piling coconut husks near nurseries.

#### Chemical control
**Recommended Insecticides and Dosages:**
1.  Imidacloprid 20% (Admire SL 200): 1-2 ml in one liter of water.
2.  Chlorpyrifos 20%: 3-5 ml in one liter of water. (If using Chlorpyrifos 40%, halve the dosage).

**Methods of Application:**
- **Pre-planting:** Dip the seed nuts in the recommended solution for 3 minutes prior to planting.
- **Post-transplanting:** Drench the soil with the insecticidal solution using a watering can. Use 5 liters of solution per square meter of the area. Treatment is crucial during the nursery stage and first years of growth.
- **Adult palms:** Drench 5 liters of the recommended solution around the palm base if attacked.

**Note:** All insecticides are toxic and should be handled with care.
