# How to Manage Animal Husbandry in Coconut Cultivation?

Animal husbandry can be integrated to increase land productivity, obtain organic manure, and reduce weed management costs.
Ruminants like cattle, buffaloes, goats, and sheep are commonly raised. Pigs and poultry can also be successfully managed. Animals efficiently control weeds, and applying their manure systematically enhances the nutritional status of the soil and crops.

Besides income from animal products, weed control and obtaining high-quality organic manure from the land itself are exceptional benefits of this integrated system. There is also high potential for growing fodder grass under coconuts, providing further advantages when combined with animal husbandry.
Integrating beekeeping with coconut cultivation ensures successful pollination, potentially increasing crop yields.
