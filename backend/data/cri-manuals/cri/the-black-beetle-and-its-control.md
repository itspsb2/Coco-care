# The Black Beetle and its Control

### Official CRI Source
- Source ID: B4
- Category: Pests & Diseases
- Title: The Black Beetle and its Control
- PDF: https://cri.gov.lk/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/b4.pdf

### Details
The Black beetle or Rhinoceros beetle (*Oryctes rhinoceros*) is a major pest in all coconut growing areas. While its damage to adult palms is not serious, it causes considerable retardation of growth in young palms and seedlings, and occasional death of seedlings.

##### Symptoms
Damage is caused by the adult beetle boring into the soft areas at the base of the bud and feeding on soft tissues. This results in damage to folded leaves and petioles.
- When leaves unfold, they exhibit characteristic geometric cuts.
- Extensive petiole damage can cause breaking of the flag leaf.
- Damage often causes choking of developing leaves in seedlings, resulting in crooked and malformed leaves.
- Damage to the growing tip of a seedling is fatal.

##### Description and Life Cycle
- **Adult Beetle:** Black, 30-40 mm long, with a prominent backwardly directed tapering horn on the head. Lives for 3-4 months.
- **Eggs:** Small, whitish, globular eggs laid in decaying organic matter (e.g., coconut logs, manure heaps, fiber dust heaps).
- **Grub (Larva):** Whitish body with three pairs of legs and a dark brown head. Has a characteristic 'C' shape when resting. Fully grown grub is about 60 mm long. The grub stage lasts about 3 months.
- **Pupa:** The grub makes a hard spherical cocoon using soil and organic matter. The pupal stage lasts about 3-4 weeks.

##### Control of the pest
Integration of all possible control measures is essential.

###### 1. Mechanical Method
- Examine seedlings and young plantations frequently.
- Look for chewed-up fibrous matter ('frass') at the base of the bud and around entry holes.
- If fresh frass is present, the adult beetle is likely inside. Remove it using a pointed metal hook. This is compulsory for plantations near fiber mills, sawdust heaps, and coconut rafter processing centers.

###### 2. Cultural Methods
- Remove and burn decaying coconut logs and stumps.
- Properly dispose of breeding media like organic manure heaps and coir dust heaps, or earth them up well with soil if used as manure.
- Regularly check mulch (decaying husk and coconut leaves) for grubs and replace it if necessary.

###### 3. Chemical Methods
- **Repellents:** Apply coal tar or used engine oil on leaf axils around the bud region. Repeat at 1-2 month intervals. Do not pour engine oil into the bud.
- **Naphthalene balls:** Place 6 balls into each of the innermost leaf axils (suitable for home gardens).
- **Systemic Granular Insecticides:** Apply if damage is severe.
    - *Carbofuran 3%:* 15 g for seedlings, 30 g for young palms.
    - *Carbosulfan 10%:* 10 g for seedlings, 15 g for young palms.
- **Application Method:** Mix the recommended dosage with an equal amount of sand and place the mixture into the leaf axils around the bud. Repeat at monthly intervals.

**Note:** All insecticides are toxic and should be handled with care.
