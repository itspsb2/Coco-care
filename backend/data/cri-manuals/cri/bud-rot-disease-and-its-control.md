# Bud Rot Disease and its Control

### Official CRI Source
- Source ID: B9
- Category: Pests & Diseases
- Title: Bud Rot Disease and its Control
- PDF: https://cri.gov.lk/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/b9.pdf

### Details
Bud rot is a fatal disease of coconut caused by the fungus *Phytophthora palmivora*, which attacks the growing point of the palm. Once the growing point is attacked, the palm is beyond recovery.

- **Susceptibility:** Palms of age 3-45 years are particularly susceptible.
- **Conditions:** The disease prevails when humidity is high, which favours the growth and dispersal of the fungus. Young palms with clustered leaves and those shaded by older palms are susceptible. The disease is often seen on river banks subject to flooding and sporadically in widely scattered palms.
- **Epidemiology:** In Sri Lanka, the disease is not widespread, but occasionally, in the wet and intermediate zones, minor epidemics have been recorded.

##### Symptoms
- Initially, the spear leaf loses its luster and withers.
- The wilting gradually spreads to adjacent leaves.
- Close examination of the bud region reveals discoloured patches at the base of the spear leaf.
- Withered fronds turn brown and dry while the lower fronds remain healthy and green.
- Drying of inflorescence and immature nut fall occurs at this stage.
- In the advanced stage, the spear leaf can be easily pulled out and will give a foul odour.
- Later, the bud and a few adjacent fronds fall, followed by other fronds, leaving only the trunk. The disease is often detected when the bud breaks away due to the rot. Such palms are beyond recovery.

##### Control
- **Infected young palms (advanced stage):** The crown should be cut and burnt to destroy the fungus.
- **If detected early:** The bud region should be thoroughly wetted with Bordeaux mixture or 1% copper fungicide solution.
- **Older bearing palms:** It is possible to keep the bunches until the nuts are matured. However, it is important to wet the bud region with a recommended chemical to kill the fungus.

##### Preventive measures
While it is difficult to cure an affected palm, preventing the spread is possible. Healthy palms surrounding affected ones should be treated as a preventive measure using Bordeaux mixture, 1% copper fungicide, or fungicidal bags.

- **Liquid Application:** When using Bordeaux mixture or copper fungicide, thoroughly wet the bud regions of healthy palms once every 2-3 weeks.
- **Fungicidal Bags:** This is a more convenient method.
    1.  Take a piece of jute, hussian, or gunny (15 cm square).
    2.  Place a handful of coir dust on this piece and tie it up to make a small bag.
    3.  Leave the bags overnight in a 1% copper fungicide solution.
    4.  Dry the bags for a few hours on the following morning.
    5.  Place one bag in the axil of the youngest leaf.
    6.  Check the bag monthly and shift it to the base of the newly emerged spear leaf.
- If heavy rain is regular, dip the bags more often. In dry weather, the bags maintain fungicidal properties for 4-6 months.

**Preparation of Bordeaux Mixture:** (Use earthenware vessels)
- Copper sulphate: 200 g
- Quick lime: 200 g
- Water: 25 litres

Dissolve copper sulphate overnight in 5 litres of water (suspend in a bag to facilitate dissolving). Suspend lime separately in 20 litres of water, and strain through a fine cloth. Add the copper sulphate to the lime, stirring vigorously. Use immediately after preparation.

**Copper Fungicides:** A large number of brands are available. Any one of these may be used as directed by the manufacturer.

**Note:** Both Bordeaux mixture and copper fungicides are toxic and should be handled with care.
