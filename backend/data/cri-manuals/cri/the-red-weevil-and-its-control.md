# The Red Weevil and its Control

### Official CRI Source
- Source ID: B3
- Category: Pests & Diseases
- Title: The Red Weevil and its Control
- PDF: https://cri.gov.lk/wp-content/uploads/2021/10/b3.pdf

### Details
The Red Weevil (*Rhynchophorus ferrugineus*) is the most serious pest of young coconut palms aged 3 to 15 years and is found in all coconut growing areas.

##### Causes for red weevil damage
Red weevils lay eggs only on fresh wounds. The adult weevil does not make wounds itself. They are attracted to fresh wounds caused by animals (e.g., cattle, wild boar), black beetle, human action, or natural cracks on the growing stem caused by enlargement of the stem and emergence of aerial roots due to a high water table.

##### Symptoms
Damage is caused by the grubs (larvae) feeding on internal tissues of the trunk, causing either the crown or trunk to fall, killing the palm. Young palms (3-15 years) are more prone to attack, and palms with large bases are more susceptible.

**Signs of infestation:**
- Holes (1.25 - 2 cm) in the crown or trunk from which chewed-up fibers are thrown out.
- Oozing of a brown viscous fluid and discoloration of tissues below the holes.
- Crunching noise of feeding grubs can be heard by placing an ear on the trunk.
- Yellowing of leaves.
- Withering and slanting of the bud.
- Presence of fibrous cocoons at the bases of petioles and beneath the bark.

##### Description of the pest
- **Adult weevil:** Reddish brown, about 36 mm long and 10 mm broad, characterized by a long snout on the head. Dark spots are visible on the dorsal middle part of the body. Females are attracted by the smell of fermenting sap from wounds.
- **Eggs:** Numerous minute, creamy white, oblong eggs laid in fresh wounds on petioles and trunk. Hatch in 3-5 days.
- **Grub (Larva):** Bores into and feeds on fibrous tissues. Grows up to 35 mm long, recognized by a brown head and white legless body.
- **Pupa:** After about 55 days, the grub makes a fibrous cocoon near the outer surface. The pupal stage inside the cocoon lasts about 20 days.
- **Adult Emergence:** The adult emerges by cutting a hole in the trunk or petioles. Emerging males secrete an aggregation pheromone that attracts both males and females, allowing a second generation to begin in the same palm.

##### Prevention
Early detection is difficult; therefore, prevention is emphasized.
- **Regular inspection:** Trained people should inspect young palms (3-15 years) for fresh wounds and early presence of the weevil.
- **Wound treatment:** Apply coal tar or used engine oil on fresh wounds on trunks and petioles to prevent egg-laying.
- **Stem bleeding:** Treat bleeding wounds with a recommended fungicide.
- **Black beetle:** Prevent black beetle breeding, as red weevil lays eggs on wounds made by black beetle.
- **Severely damaged palms:** Cut, split, and burn severely damaged palms completely to destroy all remaining grubs in the basal region.
- **General care:** Avoid damaging palms with machinery and tools. Treat cattle damage to the crown with coal tar or used engine oil.

##### Control
Infested palms should be treated with a systemic insecticide. Treatment is only effective in early stages. If the bud is withering, the palm should be cut, split, and burnt immediately.

**Recommended Insecticide:** Monocrotophos 60% at a dosage of 20 ml/palm.
- *Caution: A teaspoonful is about 5 ml. Do not use the measuring spoon for any domestic purpose. Monocrotophos 60% is highly toxic and banned for other agricultural purposes. It is issued under the supervision of Coconut Development Officers.*

**Method of Application:**
1.  **Trunk injection:** For palms with a fully developed trunk.
2.  **Pouring into bud:** For very young palms without a fully developed trunk.
3.  **Root feeding.**

###### Trunk Injection method
**Steps for Trunk Injection (Standard Method):**
1.  Pick all mature nuts before application.
2.  Make two holes of 13 mm diameter on opposite sides of the trunk, about 1 m above ground, at a 45° angle, to a depth of 10 cm.
3.  Pour 10 ml of undiluted Monocrotophos 60% into each hole using a syringe.
4.  Two days later, seal the hole with a cement and sand mixture and apply coal tar.
5.  Display a warning sign.
6.  Do not consume nuts from treated palms until 45 days after injection.

###### New Method of Trunk Injection
This method minimizes trunk damage using a smaller hole.
- **Materials:** Drill machine (with 4-5 mm bit), 25 ml plastic syringe, cap for needle.
- **Preparation:** Remove the needle, drill a small hole at the top end of the syringe, cut the tip of the needle cap to create an opening, and fix the cap to the syringe end.
- **Method:** Drill a hole 4-5 mm in diameter, 2.5-3.75 cm deep, at a 45° angle downwards, within 15 cm from ground level in healthy tissue. Suck 20 ml of Monocrotophos into the syringe, insert the cap into the hole, and fix firmly. Remove the syringe after the insecticide is absorbed (about 2 days). Seal the hole with candle wax.

###### For Young Palms (No fully formed trunk)
Mix 10 ml of Monocrotophos 60% with 500 ml of water and pour into the crown to thoroughly wet the bud region.

###### Root Feeding Method
1.  Harvest mature nuts.
2.  Expose a healthy new root (pencil thickness, light brown in colour).
3.  Mix 20 ml of Monocrotophos 60% with an equal amount of water.
4.  Transfer the mixture to a container (polythene bag, glass tube, or narrow bottle).
5.  Cut the root with a sharp knife just above the tip and insert the cut edge into the insecticide liquid.
6.  If not absorbed by the next day, transfer to a new root. Protect the area from damage.
7.  Do not consume nuts for two months from the time of treatment.

##### Management using Pheromone Trap
Recommended for estates over 5 acres.
- **Trap Setup:** A plastic bucket with a pheromone vial and a bait container. Pour 3 inches (7.5 cm) of dilute soap solution into the bucket. Use bait like sugar solution, toddy, or yeast sugar solution. Break the pheromone vial neck and tie it to the bucket handle at mouth level.
- **Density:** 5-6 traps per hectare.
- **Placement:** Tie on trunks in old plantations, or use stakes in young plantations.
- **Maintenance:** Clean and refill with fresh soap solution and bait every two weeks. Repeat the method every 5-6 months if pest density is high. Stop if the population is under control.

**Yeast Sugar Solution Recipe:** 150 g Sugar, 2 g Yeast, and 1 liter of water. Use 100 ml per trap as bait.
