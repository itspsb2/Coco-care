# How Should These Seedlings Be Planted?

* When cultivating coconuts as a pure stand (monocrop) in the field, the recommendation for any cultivar is to maintain a spacing of **26 ft × 26 ft** between seedlings (64 seedlings per acre).
* This spacing can also be recommended for coconut cultivation with annual, semi-perennial, and perennial intercrops that have low competition with coconuts.
* When intercropping with coconuts, if you expect a higher yield from perennial crops, you can maintain a spacing of **26 ft × 32 ft** (52 seedlings per acre) to increase the extent of intercrops.
* If coconuts are planted for shade in a tea land, a spacing of **26 ft × 40 ft** is recommended.
* If coconuts are planted in a cinnamon land, a spacing of **26 ft × 32 ft** is recommended.
