# How to Plant a Coconut Seedling Correctly?

To achieve attractive and rapid growth from a planted seedling, the soil must be prepared in the following manner to provide it with a favorable environment:

1.  If the field has hard soil like gravel or laterite (cabook), pits of $4 	imes 4 	imes 4$ ft should be prepared. If the land has normal sandy loam soil, pits of $3 	imes 3 	imes 3$ ft should be prepared.
2.  When preparing the pit, the topsoil removed up to about 1 ft should be placed on one side of the pit, and the subsoil on the other side.
3.  Place a layer of coconut husks at the bottom of the pit with the fiber side facing up, and apply a layer of topsoil over it.
4.  Then, add another layer of coconut husks with the fiber side facing up, and put all the remaining topsoil on top. About 30-35 coconut husks are sufficient for one pit.
5.  The subsoil should be mixed with 20 kg of dried cow dung OR 15 kg of goat manure, 1 kg of Eppawala Rock Phosphate (ERP), and 1 kg of Dolomite.
6.  Fill the remainder of the pit with this mixture and plant the seedling in it.

**If preparing this mixture using inorganic fertilizers:** Mix the subsoil and organic manure (10 kg dried cow dung / 5 kg goat manure) with:
* **i)** 250g of Urea, 250g of Muriate of Potash, and 1 kg of Dolomite. Add 750g of Eppawala Rock Phosphate (ERP) for the Wet and Intermediate zones, OR 350g of Triple Super Phosphate (TSP) for the Dry zone.
* **ii) OR**, with 1 kg of Dolomite, apply 1250g of YPM-W for the Wet and Intermediate zones, OR 850g of YPM-D for the Dry zone.

*Mulch the planted seedling properly.*
