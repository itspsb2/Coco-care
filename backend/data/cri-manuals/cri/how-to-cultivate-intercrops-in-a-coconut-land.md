# How to Cultivate Intercrops in a Coconut Land?

Most coconut land owners in Sri Lanka maintain monocultures, which lowers land productivity and is inefficient land management. Cultivating one or more suitable intercrops alongside coconut is recognized as an alternative solution to maximize land utilization and economic benefits.

In lands with soil suitability classes S2, S3, S4, monocropping coconuts may not provide sufficient income to profitably maintain the plantation. Intercropping in such lands yields better income.
Although there are about 100 intercrops and farming systems suitable for coconut lands, the grower must consider agro-ecological conditions, market demand for the crop, availability of quality planting materials, age of the coconut plantation, soil nature, and the ability to supply water and nutrients before starting.

* **0-5 Years:** Pineapple, passion fruit, papaya, banana, vegetables, cassava, soybean, maize, sunflower, and medicinal plants.
* **6-20 Years:** Shade-tolerant crops like yams, kochchi, nai miris, ginger, and turmeric.
* **20 Years to Underplanting/Replanting:** Perennial or semi-perennial crops (Pepper, cinnamon, banana, pineapple, mango, cashew, ginger, passion fruit, avocado, durian, guava).
* **During Underplanting/Replanting:** Short-term annual or semi-perennial crops can be grown, as space needs to be provided for the new coconut plants.
