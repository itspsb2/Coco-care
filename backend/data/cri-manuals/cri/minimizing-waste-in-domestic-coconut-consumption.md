# Minimizing Waste in Domestic Coconut Consumption

Domestic coconut consumption in Sri Lanka is high, utilizing about 1700 - 1800 million nuts from the annual national production. This directly impacts coconut price fluctuations in the local economy.

The amount of nutrients extracted into milk depends on the extraction method. Hand squeezing extracts only about 25%, while fine grating increases this to 40%. Using mechanical methods like a blender, grinder, grinding stone, or mortar and pestle increases extraction to 50% - 60%. Thus, popularizing mechanical methods can reduce domestic consumption by 50%, raising the potential number of nuts available for the industry.

| Method | Daily Nuts per House | Daily Cost (Rs.) | Nuts Saved per Year (vs Hand Squeezing) | Money Saved per Year (vs Hand Squeezing) (Rs.) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Hand Squeezing Grated Coconut | 01 | 70 | - | - |
| Using a Blender | 1/2 | 36 | 182 | 12,400 |
| Using a Grinder | 1/2 | 36 | 182 | 12,400 |
| Dehydrated Coconut Paste | 75g* | 37.50 | 27kg | 13,700 |
| Fresh Coconut Paste | 1/2 | 36 | 182 | 12,400 |

*\*Dehydrated Coconut Rs. 500/1kg. Electricity costs are factored into the calculations.*

By saving daily expenses as shown, the cost of a blender and electricity can be recovered in less than a year. Using quality processed coconut milk, cream, paste, or milk powder saves cooking time (about 15 mins) and is financially beneficial.
Promoting processed coconut milk and white coconut oil production at the village level increases product availability and strengthens the rural economy. Wasted coconut water can be processed into value-added products for export or local markets. Leftover coconut residue (poonac) can be added to the bakery industry, reducing wheat flour imports and improving health safety.
Minimizing waste allows collected shells and husks to be easily utilized for value-added industries. Currently, 1500 million husks and 2000 million shells are used in the industry; plans are underway to collect an additional 750 million husks and 500 million shells. A mechanism is being strengthened to collect domestic waste in a way that provides financial value to the village level.
