# Kapruka Forward

The coconut tree, scientifically known as *Cocos nucifera*, is a plant abundantly found in tropical and subtropical regions. Originating around the Asian and Pacific islands, this crop has now expanded to about 92 countries. Among these countries, Sri Lanka is the fourth-largest coconut producer in the world. 

With a history of over 3000 years in Sri Lanka, 23 varieties of the coconut plant have been identified in the country so far. Among them, King Coconut (Thambili) is an endemic variety to Sri Lanka. Except for the upper parts of the Nuwara Eliya district which have lower temperatures, this crop has successfully spread across 1.1 million acres in other areas. Based on the cultivated land area, it is second only to rice cultivation. Within this land area, there are about 60 million coconut trees, yielding an annual harvest of approximately 2800 million nuts.

Since the per capita coconut consumption in Sri Lanka is at a high value of 114 nuts per year, about 70% of the aforementioned annual coconut production is spent on domestic consumption. Sri Lankans fulfill their daily fat requirements primarily, energy requirements secondarily, and 5% of their protein requirements through coconut consumption, resulting in a high per capita consumption in the country.

However, during the past few decades, there has not been a significant increase in Sri Lanka's annual coconut yield. Currently, the annual yield produced by a single tree varies between 48 - 50 nuts. Under this sub-optimal productivity, the number of nuts available for the national coconut industry is limited to about 1000 million. Therefore, there is a need to increase the annual yield of a tree to at least 80 nuts by enhancing the productivity of coconut cultivation. Accordingly, targeted plans must be prepared to contribute an additional 1000 million nuts to the export industry by the end of the year 2023. 

For this purpose, a social responsibility with unwavering commitment is assigned to all citizens connected to the coconut sector, from home gardeners to large-scale coconut planters. Through this, our goal is to transform coconut, currently Sri Lanka's third largest export plantation crop, into the primary export crop.

Simultaneously, plans have been formulated to quickly direct 300-400 million additional nuts to the industry by minimizing domestic level waste, 200-300 million nuts by minimizing disease and pest damage, and 500-600 million nuts by increasing the yield by at least one nut per bunch through the adoption of correct agronomic practices.

Furthermore, national and international awareness programs are being launched with the aims of establishing the 'Ceylon Coconut' brand globally, increasing production, minimizing waste, introducing new value-added products, and finding new markets. 

Based on scientific facts and a long-term plan to derive maximum benefit from the land, plans have been prepared using scientific methodologies and social principles to increase coconut production to 6000 million nuts by the year 2030. We cordially invite all of you to be honorable partners in this historic mission.
