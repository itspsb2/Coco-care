# Fertilizer Recommendations for Adult Coconut Trees

| Organic Fertilizer Type | Amount (kg) | Muriate of Potash (MOP) (g) | Natural Potassium Sulphate (SOP) (g) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Cow dung manure (20%-30% moisture) | 30 | 1500 | 1250 |
| Goat manure (20%-30% moisture) | 25 | 800 | 1250 |
| Poultry manure (20%-30% moisture) | 30 | 1000 | 750 |
| Agricultural Compost (25% moisture)*| 46 | 1350 | 1000 |
| Gliricidia (60%-70% moisture) | 30 | 1350 | 1000 |

**Additional essential minerals for ALL the above adult tree organic fertilizer packages:**
* **Eppawala Rock Phosphate (ERP):** 0.7 kg per tree
* **Dolomite:** 0.5 kg per tree

**Notes:**
* *The amounts of agricultural compost to be applied are calculated based on the minimum nutrient requirements stated in Sri Lanka Standards Institute specifications 1635 : 2019. You can calculate the required amount based on the nutrient content of your specific agricultural compost.*
* *Select and use the above packages for adult coconut trees based on your availability of organic matter.*

### Application Method: For Adult Trees

**(A) Surface Application:**
The entire circle at a distance of 1.8 meters (i.e., 6 ft) from the base of an adult coconut tree is the fertilizer circle (manure circle). If there are weeds in the fertilizer circle, they should be slashed before applying fertilizer. Remove any existing mulch around the tree without removing the topsoil. Spread the recommended amount of organic fertilizer evenly over the fertilizer circle. Turn the soil up to about 6 inches deep so that the fertilizer and soil mix well. After mixing, cover the fertilizer circle entirely using a mulch such as dry coconut fronds, grass clippings, or coconut husks.

**(B) Trench Application:**
Cut a trench 3 ft wide and 2 ft deep at a distance of 3 ft from the tree. Apply the organic fertilizer into the trench, cover it with soil, and apply a thin layer of mulch.

*The recommended fertilizer amounts should be applied annually according to the recommended methods for both seedlings and bearing trees.*

---

*Note: These recommendations may be revised with the introduction of new trends in organic fertilizers currently emerging, such as liquid fertilizers and bio-fertilizers.*
