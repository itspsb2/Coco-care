# Controlling Major Diseases in Coconut Cultivation

### Weligama Coconut Leaf Wilt Disease
Reported in Weligama in 2006, this is caused by a Phytoplasma pathogen and has spread to most divisions in the Southern Province.
* **Symptoms:** Leaflets become flat and bend like ribs; lower fronds turn yellow (rarely middle fronds too); margins of yellowed leaflets dry up.
* **Control:** Identify infected trees, remove them from the field, and burn the crown to prevent spreading to other areas.

### Bud Rot Disease
A fatal disease caused by the fungus *Phytophthora palmivora*. 
* **Symptoms:** The spear leaf loses its glossy appearance, wilts, and the wilting spreads to surrounding fronds. Brown patches appear on leaflets in the bud area. In severe stages, the bud easily pulls out by hand and emits a foul odor. The wilted bud and fronds dry out, and the bud collapses.
* **Control:** Often identified only after the apical bud is destroyed, making the tree unsalvageable. Such trees MUST be cut and burned to destroy the fungus. If identified early, separate the petioles and thoroughly drench the bud with Bordeaux mixture OR a fungicide containing Dithiocarbamate (5-6 g/L) OR Metalaxyl (4 g/L) OR Tebuconazole (4 ml/L). Placing a coir dust pouch soaked in these fungicides between the petioles close to the bud can also prevent the disease.

### Leaf Spot Disease
Caused by parasitic fungi *Pestalotiopsis palmarum* and *Bipolaris incurvata*, common in seedlings and young plantations.
* **Symptoms:** Initially, small yellowish-brown spots appear on leaves. They enlarge, turn brown, merge, form large brown patches, and dry out the leaf. Symptoms are mostly on lower fronds.
* **Control:** Common in weak trees, so applying fertilizer at recommended intervals is mandatory. If the disease appears in fertilized lands, apply MOP (60% $K_2O$) 250g OR 5kg of kitchen ash for a seedling/young tree, and MOP 500g OR 10kg kitchen ash for an adult tree. Apply every 6 months during wet weather until symptoms disappear. If it doesn't reduce after applying chemical fertilizer, consider applying a fungicide.
