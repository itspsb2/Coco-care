# Basal Fertilizer Mixture

- Source ID: KAPRUKA

### Details
| Fertilizer Type | Amount (kg) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Cow dung (20% - 30% moisture) **OR** Goat manure (20% - 30% moisture) | 20 kg (Cow dung) **OR** 15 kg (Goat manure) |
| Eppawala Rock Phosphate (ERP) | 1 kg |
| Dolomite | 1 kg |

##### Applying this Fertilizer Mixture when Planting a Coconut Seedling:
1. The size of the planting pit should be selected based on the soil type of the land. For sandy loam soils, cut a $3 	imes 3 	imes 3$ ft pit. For gravelly or laterite soils, cut a $4 	imes 4 	imes 4$ ft pit.
2. When digging the pit, the topsoil up to about 1 ft depth should be piled on one side, and the remaining subsoil on the other side.
3. Since topsoil is more fertile, it should be used first when filling the pit. As deep soil is less fertile, it must be conditioned before being used to fill the rest of the pit.
4. About 30-35 coconut husks are required per pit. When filling the pit, first place a layer of coconut husks at the bottom with the fiber side facing up, and apply a layer of topsoil over it.
5. Place a second layer of coconut husks (fiber side up) and add all the remaining topsoil over it. Coconut husks can retain water up to 6-7 times their weight, and this water is released into the soil during dry periods, allowing the roots to absorb it. Furthermore, raw coconut husks contain the nutrient Potassium.
6. Mix the remaining subsoil with 20 kg of dried cow dung (20%-30% moisture) **or** 15 kg of goat manure (20%-30% moisture), plus 1 kg of Eppawala Rock Phosphate (ERP) and 1 kg of Dolomite, and use this mixture to fill the rest of the pit.
7. The pit should be filled up to a level of 20 cm below the ground level. However, in waterlogged lands, the pit should be filled up to the ground level.

* After planting a seedling in a pit prepared this way in the field, the recommended amount of organic fertilizers for seedlings must be applied systematically until the flowering stage. This greatly supports the optimal growth of the seedling, ensuring it flowers on time and provides a proper yield.
* The fertilizer recommendations for coconut seedlings and the radius of the fertilizer circle based on the age of the seedling are detailed below.
* **Fertilizer application should be done after heavy monsoon rains when there is good moisture in the soil.**
