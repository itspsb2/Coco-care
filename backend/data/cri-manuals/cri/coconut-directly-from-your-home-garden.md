# Coconut Directly from Your Home Garden

Even in a small home garden, you can plant one or a few coconut trees to fulfill your daily coconut needs. Since space is limited, choose a sunlit, unshaded spot, considering the aesthetics of the garden and buildings.
The CRI has introduced high-yielding hybrid varieties suitable for home gardens that bear fruit in 3-4 years. It is vital to pay attention to the Kapruka Handbook guidelines to plant and care for them correctly to get a successful harvest. Be vigilant against pests and diseases and follow recommended practices.

Additionally, cut a 2ft wide and 6-inch deep trench (circle) around the tree, about 2ft away from the base, forming an earth mound around it. Periodically add household leaf litter, animal manure, ash, and kitchen waste to this trench to supply nutrients and control soil drying.
Whenever you water your flower garden during dry periods, watering the coconut trench can increase the nut yield. Directing daily wastewater from kitchens and bathing areas to the coconut root zone via a surface pipe easily boosts growth and yield. 
By properly planning limited space, you can intercrop vegetables and fruits between coconut trees to meet household needs. Growing a coconut tree at home brings both economic benefits and mental satisfaction.
