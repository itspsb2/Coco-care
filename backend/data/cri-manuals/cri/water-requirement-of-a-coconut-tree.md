# Water Requirement of a Coconut Tree

An adult coconut tree requires about 40 - 60 liters of water per day. Since practically supplying this daily requirement is difficult, growers must ensure soil moisture is conserved.
In zones with an annual rainfall of over 1200 mm, the tree's water requirement can be met through moisture conservation. Methods include mulching the fertilizer circle, burying coconut husks, growing cover crops, cutting trenches, and rainwater harvesting. Besides moisture conservation, these methods provide benefits like adding organic matter to the soil, nutrient retention, increased microbial activity, temperature control, weed management, and improved soil quality.

An evenly distributed rainfall throughout the year is most favorable. When dry periods extend beyond two months, better results can be achieved by using an irrigation system. 
To conserve rainwater, small ponds or catchments should be constructed in the lower areas of the land to collect excess water during rainy seasons, preventing it from going to waste, and mitigating drought impacts later. 
When irrigation is required, the suitable method should be decided based on land topography, soil nature, water source, climate factors, active root system, and technological availability. Successful methods include hose irrigation, basin irrigation, pot irrigation, sprinkler irrigation, micro-irrigation (surface and subsurface), and hose-sprinkler irrigation.
