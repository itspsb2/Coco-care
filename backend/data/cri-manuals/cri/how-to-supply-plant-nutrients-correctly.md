# How to Supply Plant Nutrients Correctly?

### Fertilizer Types That Can Be Used for Coconuts
* To supply the recommended **Nitrogen (N)**, you can use Urea or solid organic sources like cow dung, goat manure, poultry manure, green manures like Gliricidia, and materials like compost.
* To supply **Phosphorus (P)**, Eppawala Rock Phosphate (ERP) should be used for the Wet and Intermediate zones, and Triple Super Phosphate (TSP) for the Dry zone.
* To supply **Magnesium (Mg)**, Dolomite should be used as recommended.
* Although coconut husks, wild sunflower, banana trunks, and animal manures contain some **Potassium (K)**, it is insufficient for the growth requirements of the coconut tree. Therefore, Potassium sources like Muriate of Potash (MOP), natural Potassium Chloride (KCl), or natural Sulphate of Potash (SOP) must be used.

Since the nutrient requirement of a coconut tree changes according to its growth stages, the Coconut Research Institute has introduced recommendations based on primary sources for each stage. For this, a fertilizer combination where field-available organic sources are mixed with fertilizers containing other essential nutrients as recommended can be used.

### Fertilizer Recommendations for the Seedling Stage
There are recommendations prepared as straight fertilizers or fertilizer mixtures using Urea, ERP/TSP, and MOP to meet the N, P, K requirements for the seedling stage.
* The recommended inorganic fertilizer mixtures are introduced as **YPM-W** for the Wet and Intermediate zones, and **YPM-D** for the Dry zone.
* When using straight or mixed fertilizer recommendations, the recommended amounts of Dolomite must be applied to supply Magnesium (Mg).
* Additionally, recommendations have been introduced as organic and inorganic fertilizer mixtures, which should be applied to seedlings as indicated in the tables below.

### Fertilizer Recommendations for Bearing Coconut Trees
For bearing trees, there are recommendations as straight fertilizers or mixtures using Urea, ERP/TSP, and MOP.
* **Straight inorganic recommendation for a bearing tree:** 800g Urea, 1600g MOP, 1000g Dolomite, PLUS 900g ERP (for Wet/Intermediate zones) OR 400g TSP (for Dry zone).
* **If using mixtures:** Apply 3.3 kg of **APM-W** (Wet/Intermediate) OR 2.8 kg of **APM-D** (Dry zone) per tree, along with 1 kg of Dolomite.
* Depending on the organic materials easily available in the field, the following recommendations can also be used for bearing trees.

| Organic Fertilizer Type | Amount (kg) | MOP (g) | *OR* Natural Pot. Sulphate (g) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Cow dung (20-30% moisture) | 30 | 1250 | 1500 |
| Goat manure (20-30% moisture) | 25 | 800 | 1250 |
| Cured poultry manure (20-30% moisture) | 30 | 750 | 1000 |
| Gliricidia (60-70% moisture) | 30 | 1000 | 1350 |

*(For the above organic packages, add 0.7 kg ERP and 0.5 kg Dolomite per tree).*

* **For improved and hybrid cultivars**, and in cases where a coconut tree yields **more than 75 nuts per year**, 1.5 times the above-mentioned nutrient amounts should be applied per tree.
* Agricultural compost can be used with other essential nutrient fertilizers. However, since the nutritional composition of compost varies significantly based on the raw materials, a general recommendation cannot be provided. Recommendations can be issued after analyzing the compost composition.
* Applying salt has become a traditional practice among growers, believing it prevents drooping of fronds and bunches. Since applying salt can increase nitrogen retention in the soil, applying 1 kg of common salt (NaCl - non-iodized or non-mud salt) per adult tree annually alongside the fertilizer recommendation is advantageous to the grower.

The recommended fertilizer amounts should be applied once a year based on the age of the tree, according to the recommended methods. The period when soil moisture is retained after heavy rains is suitable for fertilization. If irrigation is available, fertilizers can be applied at any time of the year by moistening the soil.

### How to Apply Fertilizer to a Coconut Tree

| Age of Seedling/Tree | Distance from Base (Fertilizer Circle Radius) |
| :--- | :--- |
| 06 Months | 30 cm (1 ft) |
| 01 Year | 60 cm (2 ft) |
| 02 Years | 90 cm (3 ft) |
| 03 Years | 120 cm (4 ft) |
| 04 Years to Flowering | 150 cm (5 ft) |
| Bearing Trees | 180 cm (6 ft) |

Fertilizer application can be done in two ways: surface application or trench application.

**Surface Application:**
Spread the recommended fertilizer amount evenly all over the fertilizer circle (based on the age of the tree) and fork the soil to a depth of 4-6 inches so the fertilizer mixes well with the soil. Afterward, mulch the fertilized area with dry coconut fronds, husks, grass clippings, or slashed weeds. Previously applied mulch should be raked away before applying new fertilizer. If weeds are present in the circle, slash them and remove them without removing the topsoil (limit the use of hoes to prevent topsoil loss).

**Trench Application:**
Cut a trench 3 ft wide and 1/2 ft deep at a distance of 3 ft from the tree. Spread the selected fertilizer mixture in the trench, cover it with soil, and mulch the fertilizer circle. When mulching, ensure a 15 cm radius circle around the base of the tree is left uncovered.
