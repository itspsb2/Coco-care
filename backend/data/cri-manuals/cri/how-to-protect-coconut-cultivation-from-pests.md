# How to Protect Coconut Cultivation from Pests?

### Black Beetle (Kalu Kuruminiya) Damage
Adult black beetles bore into the cabbage (bud) area of the trees and feed on tender parts, damaging unrolled fronds and spathes, which appear as if cut by scissors when they unfurl. While damage to adult trees doesn't significantly affect growth, in seedlings and immature trees, it causes a significant growth reduction. Furthermore, damage to the apical bud in small seedlings can even cause the seedling to die.

**To prevent Black Beetle damage:**
* Beetles lay eggs in cow dung, coir dust, decaying husk piles, and decaying logs. Do not leave such materials piled up in the field for long periods. If trunk parts remain after cutting trees, uproot and burn them.
* Inspect small seedlings frequently (at least weekly). If fresh fibers or frass are found around holes at the base or bud area, use a wire hook (beetle hook) to extract the beetles.
* Wrapping a piece of fishnet with small holes around the bud and between the petioles prevents beetles from reaching the bud. This protects seedlings up to about 4 years of age; loosen and move the net up as the seedling grows.
* Place 2 mothballs (camphor balls) inside the leaf axils of small seedlings (about 6 balls per seedling) to repel beetles.
* Apply burnt oil (used engine oil without kerosene) around the petioles in the bud area and on any cuts/splits on the stem.
* Wrap a wire mesh (chicken wire) around the seedling to protect from beetles, and bury it about 6 inches into the ground to protect against porcupines.
* If damage is severe and adult insects are seen, use pheromone traps (1 trap per 5 acres for lands >5 acres). Place them away from the seedling area or on boundary fences. Change the pheromone every 3-4 months.
* For severe damage, use a systemic granular insecticide.

### Red Weevil (Rathu Kuruminiya) Damage
This pest affects plantations between 3-15 years of age. If not controlled in time, it causes fatal damage. The larval stage feeds on the stem tissues, causing the bud to wilt and collapse, leading to the tree's death.

**How to identify damage in early stages?**
* Inspect the plantation frequently (weekly) and keep the stem area clean.
* Brownish ooze flowing outward from small holes on the stem is the first sign.
* Finding fibrous cocoons where petioles attach to the stem, yellowing of fronds, the bud wilting and leaning to one side, and hearing the sound of larvae feeding when putting an ear to the trunk are other signs. A Red Weevil detection device can be used to hear the sound clearly.
* Red weevils lay eggs on fresh wounds on the tree or exposed tender parts of petioles. Properly preventing Black Beetle damage greatly prevents Red Weevil damage.
* Apply burnt oil on cuts/splits; treat any oozing points; remove and burn heavily damaged trees.
* Pheromone traps can be used (2 traps per acre) when pest population is very high. Change the pheromone every 3-4 months.
* **Chemical Control:** Monocrotophos 60 SL is recommended. For a tree with a hidden trunk, mix 10ml insecticide with 500ml water and pour it into the crown to wet the bud area. For trees with a clear trunk: If base girth is <100cm, use 30ml; if >100cm, use 40ml. Select two healthy tissue spots on opposite sides of the damaged tree, drill two holes (10cm deep, 13mm diameter) at a 45-degree angle, and inject the undiluted insecticide equally into both holes using a syringe. 
* Alternatively, use Thiamethoxam 20% + Chlorantraniliprole 20% mixture (Voliam Flexi 40 WG). Dissolve 4g in 250ml water. Inject 60ml of this solution equally into two holes drilled similarly near the damaged area. If the trunk is taller than 5 ft, drill two more holes above the 5ft mark and inject another 60ml.
* Seal holes with a sand-cement mixture a week after application. Also, seal any holes caused by the weevil damage. If oozing persists, drill new holes after 2 weeks and reapply.

### Coconut Mite (Pol Maita) Damage
An invasive pest to Sri Lanka (*Aceria guerreronis*), this microscopic organism lives under the bracts (calyx) of the coconut and sucks sap from soft tissues.

**Identifying Mite Damage:**
Nuts aged 3-6 months are mostly infected. Initially, a small yellow/white triangular patch appears below the calyx. Continuous sap-sucking causes this patch to enlarge and turn brown as the nut grows. Later, the nut surface cracks and dries, stunting growth, and causing deformed and split nuts.

**Minimizing Mite Damage:**
* Complete eradication is difficult, but it can be minimized by spraying a Palm/Vegetable oil and Sulfur mixture. Mix 12g soap powder and 5g wettable sulfur powder in 800ml water, then add 200ml Palm/Vegetable oil and mix well (makes 1 Liter). Spray this to thoroughly wet all bunches in the crown. Spray every 6 months to all bunches except newly opened inflorescences (spraying during Feb and July-Aug is recommended).
* **Biological Control:** The CRI recommends a predatory mite found locally. These are produced in labs and provided in small polypropylene bags. Pierce a corner of the bag and place it among the affected bunches (buttons) using a climber or a long pole. Recommendation is one packet for every four trees (e.g., if there are 100 infected trees, place 25 packets spread across the plantation). Applying this at the end of the rainy season yields better results. Apply continuously for at least two years, every 3-4 months (avoiding rainy seasons).

### Whitefly (Sudu Messa) Damage
A small white insect that sucks sap from leaflets of seedlings and adult trees. It looks like white cotton on the underside of leaves. The honeydew excreted by the whitefly settles on lower fronds, causing Sooty Mold (black fungus).
* **Chemical Control:** Dissolve 3g of Thiamethoxam OR 20ml of Carbosulfan in 10 Liters of water and spray thoroughly on the underside of the leaflets. Alternatively, dissolve 2.5g of an insecticide containing Chlorantraniliprole and Thiamethoxam in 10 Liters of water and spray.

### Plesispa Beetle Damage
Damages nursery seedlings and young field-planted seedlings, stunting growth. Leaflets turn brown and dry out, reducing nursery seedling quality. Damage is visible only after the bud opens.
* **Control:** Spray Carbosulfan 20 SC (e.g., Marshal 20 SC) - 3-4 ml per liter of water, thoroughly wetting the bud area. If insects/damage persist, respray after 3 weeks.

### Coconut Caterpillar (Pol Dalabuwa) Damage
The caterpillar feeds on the tissues on the underside of coconut leaflets, leading to significant yield reduction. Leaflets turn brown and dry out.
* If there's an epidemic, CRI can provide lab-bred parasitoids for biological control.
* **Chemical control (Seedlings/immature trees):** Spray Carbosulfan 20 SC (Marshal) 4 ml per liter of water.
* **For adult trees (if parasitoids fail during epidemics):** Inject 8 ml of Monocrotophos 60 SL into the trunk.

### Other Damage
Mammals like porcupines cause damage. Protect seedling bases with mechanical barriers like chicken wire or barrels. Repel monkeys, macaques, giant squirrels, or elephants by scaring them (firecrackers, air rifles). Use elephant fences in elephant-frequented areas.
